Shredder

Erases any unwanted files or folders without leaving a trace.

The Shredder module helps you remove any item you no longer need in either a secure or ordinary way. It deals with a task seamlessly even when Finder brings up errors while trying to remove items involved in current processes.

Select files you want to erase: Click Select Files, locate an item you want to remove and click Open. Alternatively, drag-and-drop files directly to the module.

Modify Shredder’s removal list

  • Exclude previously selected items from the removal list: Deselect checkboxes next to items you want to keep on your Mac. Alternatively, click Deselect All to exclude all items from the removal list.
  • Add new items to the removal list: Click Add Files and locate items you want to remove.

Sort results: Click the “Sort by” menu and choose an appropriate option.

Search within a list of items added for removal: Start typing a word in the search field - results appear below the field right as you type. To view all available items, click the close button (x).

Shred selected files

  • Remove immediately: Click Shred to erase files in an ordinary way. CleanMyMac uses this erase method by default.
  • Remove securely: Choose Remove Securely from the pop-up menu next to the Shred button. Click Shred to delete all selected item in a secure way. This takes more time, but CleanMyMac rewrites all data with zeroes and, therefore, makes the recovery impossible. You can be sure that deleted items will not be recovered by means of any special tools.

Note: Despite the fact that the Secure Empty Trash feature is missing in macOS El Capitan (10.11 or higher), CleanMyMac still effectively removes your data in a secure way.
